# JB Automations — Go-To-Market Plan (2026)

This plan is built around the first offer JB Automations can sell now: helping small businesses set up AI for their business without asking them to commit to a large project first.

The current strategy is simple:

1. Start local and trust-first.
2. Use the free discovery call to find fit.
3. Convert good-fit prospects into a paid AI Workflow Setup Session.
4. Let the setup session reveal the first real automation, audit, project, or retainer opportunity.

---

## Positioning

**Core message:** JB Automations helps small businesses set up AI for their business, starting with one practical workflow at a time.

**Core line:** Start building your AI Operating System, one workflow at a time.

**Plain-English explanation:** Most small businesses do not need a huge AI transformation project. They need help getting their business context, tools, and repetitive work organized so AI can actually help.

**What not to lead with yet:** Big retainers, expensive transformation projects, or vague AI strategy. Those can come later after trust and proof.

---

## Offer Ladder

| Rung | Offer | Price | Purpose |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| 0 | AI Workflow Setup Session | $150 | Set up the client's AIOS folder structure, capture business context, and identify the first useful workflow opportunity. |
| 1 | Paid audit / scoping | $500-$2,500 | Map workflows, prioritize opportunities, and define the first build. |
| 2 | Focused implementation project | $2,500-$10,000+ | Build one valuable workflow end to end. |
| 3 | Ongoing advisory / retainer | $500-$1,500+/mo to start | Stay involved as the client's AI person and keep improving the system. |

The public entry point is not "buy a project." The public entry point is:

> Book a free discovery call.

The next step for a good-fit prospect is usually:

> AI Workflow Setup Session — $150 for 60-90 minutes.

---

## AI Workflow Setup Session

**Public name:** AI Workflow Setup Session
**Internal name:** AIOS Setup Session
**Price:** $150
**Length:** 60-90 minutes
**Early rep option:** $99 founding client price when useful. Free or $50 only for warm practice sessions where the main goal is reps.

### What It Guarantees

- Clone or set up the AIOS folder structure for the client.
- Walk the client through the AIOS onboarding questions.
- Capture their business context, priorities, voice, tools, and weekly pain points.
- Explain how the AIOS works in plain language.
- Identify the first workflow or automation opportunity.
- Recommend the next step.

### What It Does Not Guarantee

- A finished automation.
- A fully mapped complex workflow.
- A complete AI transformation plan.
- A project proposal before enough context exists.

If the workflow is simple and time allows, map it. If it is complex, make it the next-step recommendation.

## Free Discovery Call

The 30-minute discovery call stays free.

Its job is to qualify the prospect and decide whether the AI Workflow Setup Session makes sense.

### Discovery Call Goals

- Understand the business.
- Find what manual work is eating time.
- Check whether the owner is curious and willing to take action.
- Explain the AIOS concept without overcomplicating it.
- Offer the $150 setup session only when there is a fit.

### Discovery Call Positioning

Use language like:

> "This first call is just to understand your business and see where AI could actually help. If it looks like there is a fit, the next step is usually a paid AI Workflow Setup Session where we set up your AI Operating System and start organizing the first workflow."

## Ideal Customer Profile

Start local-first.

| | Target |
|---|---|
| **Business size** | Small local businesses, usually owner-led or operator-led |
| **Industries first** | Professional services, hospitality, restaurants, and other local service businesses |
| **Decision maker** | Owner, operator, general manager, office manager, or department lead |
| **Core pain** | Manual repetitive work, scattered knowledge, missed follow-ups, admin drag, messy handoffs, unclear AI adoption |
| **Best-fit buyer** | Curious enough to try AI, but too busy to figure it out alone |
| **Geography** | Local first, then remote and wider markets after traction |

Manufacturing, logistics, and broader professional services can remain future targets, but the first push should be easier to explain locally.

## Channel Strategy

### Primary Motion: Local Trust

The fastest first clients are likely to come from people who can meet Johnny, hear the offer in plain English, and trust him enough to book the first working session.

Priority channels:

- Local chamber of commerce
- Local business groups
- Coffee meetings
- Direct introductions
- Referral partners
- Local professional services, hospitality, and restaurants

### Secondary Motion: Online Proof

Online marketing supports the local motion. It should make Johnny look credible when someone checks him out after a conversation.

Priority channels:

- Website
- LinkedIn profile
- LinkedIn posts
- Light email outreach after positioning is revised
- Case studies and examples

Do not start paid ads yet.

---

## Website Role

The website should not try to sell every service at once.

Its main job is to make a business owner think:

> "This sounds practical. I should book the free discovery call."

Recommended website structure:

- Lead with setting up AI for small businesses.
- Keep the main CTA as "Book a Free Discovery Call."
- Introduce the AI Workflow Setup Session as the normal next step after fit is confirmed.
- Teach the AI Operating System concept as the larger framework.
- Avoid making the $150 price the first thing people see.

- Pick the first local chamber or business group to contact.

- Update the website or pitch language based on real conversations.
- Turn the revised plan into a weekly checklist.

## Online Content Plan

Content should support the same message:

> Most businesses do not need AI hype. They need one practical place to start.

### Post Types

**1. Practical AI setup**

Explain how a business owner can start organizing business context, workflows, docs, and repetitive tasks.

**2. Workflow examples**

Show simple before/after examples:

- Manual follow-up -> drafted follow-up process
- Scattered SOPs -> AI-searchable business context
- Repetitive reports -> workflow candidate for automation
- Missed leads -> CRM/follow-up process

**3. Local business angle**

Speak to business owners who do not have a tech team and do not want a giant project.

## First Sale Roadmap

1. **Free discovery call:** Understand the business and check fit.
2. **AI Workflow Setup Session:** Set up the AIOS folder structure, capture context, and identify the first workflow opportunity.
3. **Follow-up email:** Summarize what was set up, what was learned, and the recommended next step.
4. **Next paid step:** If the opportunity is clear, offer paid audit/scoping or a small implementation project.
5. **Project:** Build one focused workflow end to end.
6. **Retainer:** After trust and results, offer ongoing AI support.

The goal is not to force a proposal after every discovery call. The goal is to earn the next rung.
