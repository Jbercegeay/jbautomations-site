# Discovery Call Guide — Free 30-Min AI Discovery Call

Use this during the call. Keep it open alongside Google Meet. You talk about 30% of the time. Your job is to listen, understand the business, and decide whether the AI Workflow Setup Session is the right next step.

The discovery call is not where you sell a big project. It is where you find fit.

---

## PHASE 1 — INTRO (2 min)

> "Thanks for taking the time today. What I want to do is spend about 25 minutes learning about your business, what's eating time, and where AI might actually help. If it looks like there is a fit, I can explain the AI Workflow Setup Session as a practical next step. Sound good?"

- Set the agenda out loud.
- Keep it relaxed.
- Make clear this is not a hard sales pitch.
- The goal is fit, not a proposal.

---

## PHASE 5 — AIOS FIT CHECK (5 min)

Explain the concept plainly:

> "The way I think about this is an AI Operating System. That sounds bigger than it is. It just means we organize your business context, tools, files, and workflows in one place so AI can start helping with real work instead of generic answers."

Look for these signals:

- They have repeated manual work.
- Important knowledge is scattered or stuck in someone's head.
- They are curious about AI but do not know where to start.
- They are willing to sit down and work through setup.
- They have at least one workflow that could become a future automation.

## PHASE 6 — NEXT STEP (3 min)

Pick the right path based on the call.

**If they are a good fit for the setup session:**

> "Based on what you've told me, I think the right next step is an AI Workflow Setup Session. It's a 60-90 minute working session where we set up your AI Operating System folder structure, capture your business context, build the initial tool inventory, and identify the first connector that would make the system more useful. After that, I'll recommend the best next paid step, whether that's a connector setup, a workflow build, a knowledge base, a dashboard, or ongoing support."

Then introduce the price:

> "That session is $150. It is meant to be a low-risk first step before we talk about connector setup, automation projects, dashboards, or retainers."

**If they need more thinking:**

> "Let me think through what I heard and follow up with a short summary and recommendation. I do not want to push you into the wrong next step."

**If there is a clear project but not enough context yet:**

> "There may be a real automation opportunity here, but I would not scope it from a 30-minute call. I would start with the setup session so we can get your business context organized, identify the first connector, and define the right next step properly."

**If they are not a fit:**

> "I do not think I would recommend a paid session yet. I would start by getting [specific missing piece] clear first. I'm happy to point you in the right direction."

**Closing line:**

> "I'll send you a follow-up email today with a summary of what we discussed and the next step I recommend. Does that work for you?"

## SETUP SESSION DELIVERY PROMISE

Use this language when needed:

> "The setup session does not promise a finished automation or live connector setup. It promises the foundation: your AIOS folder structure, your business context captured, your initial tool inventory, and a clear recommendation for the first connector and next paid step."

If the workflow is simple and time allows, map it. If it is complex, recommend it as the next paid step.
